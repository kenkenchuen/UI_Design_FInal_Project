Lesson 4 - Quantifiers

Quantifiers are indicated by curly braces, and are used to specify how many repititions of a character are desired. For example, “a{5}” will match the string "aaaaa". A range of repititions can be specified by putting two numbers seperated by a comma inside the curly braces. For example, “b{3,5}” will match "b" at least 3 times and at most 5 times. Thus, it will match "bbb", "bbbb", and "bbbbb". Quantifiers can be used with metacharacters as well; ".{2,4}" will match between 2 and 4 of any character, and "[abc]{3}" will match 3 characters, each of which can be a, b, or c.
