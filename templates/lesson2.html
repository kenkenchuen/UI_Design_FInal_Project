Lesson 2 - Wildcard (.)

Meta characters are the building blocks of regular expressions. Meta characters do not stand for themselves but instead are interpreted in some special way.

The simplest example of a meta character is “.” which matches any single character (letter, digit, whitespace).

Pattern
Text
".ar"
⇒
The car was parked outside.
“a.a”
⇒
The data is not avalible for public use.
⇒
Note, these examples (and future examples) will highlight all strings which match the regular expression.
Regex normally matches just the first matching string.

To move onto the next lesson, write a single regex pattern to match the strings "able" and "call" in the following sentences:
I was not able to get the limited-edition t-shirt.
Please call the office and tell Mary that we're late.
